The Method

Sonic Mirror Journey

All AstroPhonia compositions are tuned to 432Hz — a pitch standard where the A note resonates at 432 vibrations per second, slightly below the modern concert tuning of 440Hz. Used by composers across the centuries, it is often described as warmer and more grounded. Each piece is composed live on a Roland Juno-Gi and born from a real human transition: loss, solitude, displacement, beginning again. Nothing was invented. Everything was lived.

How It Works

The Journey

The Sonic Mirror is the idea that music, when composed from lived experience, can reflect something back to the listener — not as an answer, but as a companion. I translate my own transitions into sound at 432Hz so that the vibration carries the weight of what was real. You bring your own story. The music meets you there. What happens in that encounter belongs entirely to you.

432Hz · Loss & Grief

Beyond the Silence

I lost my partner during Covid, at 38, in total solitude — unable to grieve alongside anyone. I had to find my own floor when everything collapsed. This composition was born in that silence. It carries the weight of what was lost and the quiet of what came after. A sonic companion for those navigating their own grief, at their own pace.

The Uncharted Self

I left my country carrying only what I could hold inside me. This composition was born between two worlds — in the space where what you left still echoes and what lies ahead has no map yet. A sonic mirror for those in the middle of a crossing: neither here nor there, but moving.

432Hz · Finding Real Love

Love Across The Ocean

Love doesn't ask for permission or timing. This song was born from an unexpected moment — a stranger on a bus, a casual conversation, a name I almost forgot. Love found me at 49, across an ocean I hadn't planned to cross. Not because I was looking. Because it was simply meant to happen. For everyone who stopped believing — it might already be on its way.

432Hz · Sound Relief

Exiety

The mind can sometimes become a noisy labyrinth, a place where the chest tightens and the pace quickens. This song was born from the search for a breathing space — an escape point between the exit and anxiety. «Exiety» is not a promise of eternal calm; it is simply an alternative to the chaos. A 432Hz space for decompression. For anyone who needs to slow the world down for a few minutes — the exit might just be in the sound.
